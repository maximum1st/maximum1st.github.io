2.（It's prudent to be cautious with certain stores.）

I took my iMac for repairs to PC Dock 24 in Funabashi. When I caught the eye of the man at the counter, his bloodshot eyes made me think something was off. He bizarrely asked if he could pre-work on my device without explaining what that meant. At the time, not knowing any better, I agreed. However, repeated calls to the shop went unanswered, including one the day before and another just before opening on the day of the repair. When the man, likely the owner, emerged from the shop, his comments were off-putting and discriminatory, making me feel extremely stressed, leading to a physical reaction. His demeanor was quite unsettling. Despite the discomfort, I decided to let it go, but another bizarre and confrontational call upon my return led me to cancel the service altogether. I advise against visiting this shop; the behavior there suggests a duality in character that's quite perplexing. It felt as though the owner doesn't respect his customers, taking them for granted. I'm mentioning this to record my experience. Regardless of the situation, one expects a certain level of equitable service which was not provided. This was not about expecting divine intervention, just common decency and respect. I later learned it's improper to call a business before it opens, but that does not excuse the poor service I received. A business has a responsibility to explain its processes to its customers. While Smartphone Dock, which appears to be related, provided satisfactory service, this was not the case at PC Dock 24. My monitor even came loose after their repair, though they did fix it without charge. Overall, a very disappointing and frustrating experience.
